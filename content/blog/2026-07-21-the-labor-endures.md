## The Labor Endures

> If you accomplish something good with hard work, the labor passes quickly, but the good endures; if you do something shameful in pursuit of pleasure, the pleasure passes quickly, but the shame endures.
>
> — Musonius Rufus, Lectures and Fragments

Every rep, every sprint, every moment of discomfort in the gym – it’s all labor. In the thick of it, the burn feels endless, the sweat stings, and the clock crawls. But then it's over. The immediate pain fades, but the strength gained, the skill refined, the resilience built? That remains. It endures.

This isn't just about physical gains. It's about the character forged in those moments of effort. What you build through disciplined, hard work today becomes a permanent part of you, ready for tomorrow's challenges. Don't chase fleeting comfort; embrace the grind that leaves a lasting mark on your capabilities and your spirit.

**Today's challenge:** Today, pick one movement you dislike and commit to executing it with perfect form and full effort, remembering the enduring good it builds.
