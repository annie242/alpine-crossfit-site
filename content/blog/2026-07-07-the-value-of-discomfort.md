## The Value Of Discomfort

> No man is more unhappy than he who never faces adversity; for he is not permitted to prove himself.
>
> — Seneca, Moral Letters to Lucilius 13.4

Think about the last time you truly struggled in a workout. Maybe it was that final rep, the last few seconds of an AMRAP, or just showing up when you really didn't want to. That feeling of pushing past your perceived limits, of enduring discomfort, is where growth happens.

It's not just about physical gains; it's about proving to yourself what you're capable of. Embrace the friction, the burn, the mental battle. These moments aren't punishments; they are opportunities to forge resilience and discover your true strength.

**Today's challenge:** Today, when you feel the urge to back off during a challenging moment in your workout, lean into it for just five more seconds.
