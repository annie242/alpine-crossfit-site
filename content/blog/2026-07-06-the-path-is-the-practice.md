## The Path Is The Practice

> If you wish to improve, be content to be thought foolish and stupid with regard to external things.
>
> — Epictetus, Enchiridion 13

Think about that new movement, that heavier lift, or the WOD you dread. The initial attempts often feel awkward, clumsy, and far from perfect. It's easy to feel foolish when you're not instantly proficient, especially in a gym full of seasoned athletes.

But true progress isn't about looking good on day one; it's about showing up consistently and embracing the learning curve. Don't let the fear of appearing less than perfect stop you from trying, failing, and ultimately growing. Your willingness to look 'foolish' is the very thing that makes you stronger.

**Today's challenge:** Today, pick one movement you feel less confident in and dedicate a few extra minutes to practicing it without judgment.
