## The Toil Endures

> If one accomplishes some good though with toil, the toil passes, but the good remains.
>
> — Musonius Rufus, Lectures and Sayings

Every rep, every sprint, every moment of discomfort in the gym feels substantial in the moment. The burning lungs, the aching muscles — these sensations demand our full attention. Yet, Musonius Rufus reminds us that these transient pains are merely the cost of something far more lasting: the good that remains. The effort fades, but the strength, the discipline, the progress, these are permanent.

This isn't just about physical gains. It's about building character. The willingness to push through when every fiber of your being wants to quit, that's where true growth happens. You are forging resilience, piece by painful piece, creating a foundation that will serve you long after the sweat dries.

**Today's challenge:** Today, when the workout gets tough, focus on the lasting 'good' you're building, not just the fleeting 'toil'.
