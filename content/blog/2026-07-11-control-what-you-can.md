## Control What You Can

> Some things are in our control and others are not. Things in our control are opinion, pursuit, desire, aversion, and, in a word, whatever are our own actions. Things not in our control are body, property, reputation, command, and, in one word, whatever are not our own actions.
>
> — Epictetus, Enchiridion 1

Think about your workout today. You can't control the exact weight on the bar, how fast someone else finishes, or if you PR every single time. What you *can* control is your effort, your focus on technique, your attitude when things get tough, and your decision to show up.

Focusing on what's outside your sphere of influence only leads to frustration and wasted energy. Direct your energy instead toward your internal response and your actions. That's where your true power lies, and that's where you'll find genuine progress.

**Today's challenge:** Before your next set, take a breath and consciously decide to focus only on the elements of the movement you can directly control.
