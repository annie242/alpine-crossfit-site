## Effort Is Its Own Reward

> No tree is cut down in the first blow. It is by a succession of blows that it is finally brought to the ground. So too with our efforts: it is by persistence and repeated attempts that we achieve our goals.
>
> — Seneca, Letters from a Stoic 108.14

That feeling of hitting a new PR, or just finishing a brutal WOD, isn't from one perfect rep. It's the sum of every single attempt, every missed lift, every scaled movement, every time you showed up when you didn't want to. Each effort, however small, chips away at the challenge, building strength and resilience.

Don't wait for the 'big' win to feel accomplished. Recognize the value in each individual push, pull, or squat. The true reward isn't just the outcome, but the consistent, deliberate effort you put in, day after day.

**Today's challenge:** Today, pick one movement you find challenging and commit to giving it your full, focused effort for every single rep, even if it means scaling back the weight or reps slightly.
