    <h2>Is Alpine CrossFit good for beginners?</h2>
    <p>Yes. Most Alpine members started with zero CrossFit experience, and many had not stepped into a gym in years. Every class is led by a CF-L2 certified coach who scales movements to your body in real time. Nobody is thrown into something they cannot handle.</p>
    <p>Alpine is in Wheat Ridge, with members from Lakewood, Arvada, Applewood, and Golden. If you are looking for your first CrossFit gym in the northwest Denver metro, this page is for you.</p>

  <div class="container container-narrow">
    <span class="eyebrow">The path</span>
    <h2>How do I start CrossFit at Alpine?</h2>
    <p>The path from "I have never done this" to "I am in a group class with a coach who knows my name" looks like this:</p>
    <ol class="feature-list" style="list-style-type: decimal;">
      <li><strong>Free No Sweat Intro.</strong> 30 minutes with a coach. Tour the gym, talk through your goals, and leave with a recommendation. No workout, no cost, no pressure.</li>
      <li><strong>Foundations on-ramp.</strong> Private one-on-one sessions teaching the core CrossFit movements before you join a group class.</li>
      <li><strong>Group classes.</strong> Join the regular schedule, with a CF-L2 coach in every class.</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container container-narrow">
    <span class="eyebrow">Foundations</span>
    <h2>What is the Foundations program?</h2>
    <p>Foundations is a series of private one-on-one sessions where a CF-L2 coach teaches you the core CrossFit movements — squat, deadlift, press, pull, carry — in a low-pressure setting. No crowds. No confusion about what to do.</p>
    <p>You typically need three to five sessions before you are ready for group classes, but your coach makes that call based on movement quality, not a calendar.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section section-dark">
  <div class="container container-narrow">
    <span class="eyebrow">Why private first</span>
    <h2>Why do private sessions before group?</h2>
    <p>Group classes are better when you already know the basics. You move with confidence. You understand the coach's cues. You spend less mental energy on "what am I doing?" and more on effort and breathing.</p>
    <p>Foundations is an investment in your first month that pays back in safety and confidence for years.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="container container-narrow">
    <span class="eyebrow">Don't worry about it</span>
    <h2>You do not need to be in shape first.</h2>
    <p>The single most common reason people put off starting CrossFit is the worry that they need to "get in shape" first. You do not. Every movement scales down as far as needed.</p>
    <p>Your first class might be light air squats, a few step-ups, and a 20-minute walk on the rower. Your hundredth class will look completely different — because your body will be completely different. The work is the path, and you start where you are.</p>
  </div>
</section>
