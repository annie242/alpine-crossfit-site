    <h2>What Alpine doesn't offer.</h2>
    <p>Trustworthiness matters. If we're going to claim Alpine is the right fit for the right person, we should also be clear about who we're <em>not</em> the right fit for — and what we don't offer.</p>
    <ul class="feature-list">
      <li><strong>No childcare.</strong> If on-site childcare is a hard requirement, a big-box gym (Lifetime, 24 Hour Fitness) will fit better. Our class schedule is built around school drop-off and pickup windows, which works for many Alpine parents, but it's not the same as childcare.</li>
      <li><strong>No CrossFit Kids program.</strong> Alpine welcomes athletes 13 and up — teens train in the regular coached classes alongside adults, scaled appropriately. If you're looking for a dedicated kids-only program, this isn't it.</li>
      <li><strong>No yoga classes.</strong> Alpine focuses on strength and conditioning. Mobility work happens inside CrossFit class warm-ups and cool-downs, but there's no standalone yoga class on the schedule.</li>
      <li><strong>No breath-work or meditation programming.</strong> Same reason — it's not what we do. Members who want that pair Alpine with a separate practice.</li>
      <li><strong>No weekend afternoon classes.</strong> Saturday classes run 8:00–10:00 AM and Sunday class runs 9:00–10:00 AM. If a weekend afternoon class is essential, we're not it.</li>
    </ul>
    <p>If none of those are deal-breakers, Alpine is probably a great fit. If one of them is, we can usually point you to a local gym that does that thing well.</p>
